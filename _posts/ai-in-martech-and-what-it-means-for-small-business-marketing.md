# AI in MarTech and What it Means for Small Business Marketing

Marie Lu, sci-fi author whose young adult book Warcross graced the New York Times bestseller list is credited with saying, "Everything's science fiction until someone makes it science fact."

Enter artificial intelligence (AI).
No longer the stuff of sci-fi or enterprise-only innovation, AI is integral to marketing success for small and midsize businesses (SMBs).

Just how integral?

According to Martech Consulting Co-Founder & CEO, Jon Noury-Elliard, "For SMBs, AI in marketing means less guessing and more growing."

From predictive analytics to content generation, today's AI-powered tools can help small businesses automate, analyze, and act, minus the need to hire a full team of data scientists or developers. There's a caveat though. While you can get away without the developers, it does take the best martech agency to manage and elevate your AI arsenal.

Let's get into the nitty-gritty of what exactly AI might mean for your own marketing initiatives, not to mention the sustained success of your small or mid-sized business.

What Can AI Do for Your Small Business Marketing?

Not to sound obnoxious, but a better question is what can't it do?

Rather than launch into an exhaustive exploration about AI's merits, the following capabilities are where you'll really feel a difference in your business (and how you manage it).

Improved Customer Targeting
AI can identify patterns in customer behavior to help you send the right message to the right person at the right time.

Automated Repetitive Tasks
As creative people who quickly tire of rote, but critical, to-dos, we think marketing automations alone demonstrate the power and value of AI for small and midsize businesses. Think scheduling social posts, segmenting email lists, or even drafting initial ad copy. AI takes care of the tedious stuff so you can focus on strategy. No wonder Isaac Asmiov said, "I do not fear computers. I fear the lack of them." Same, sir. Same.

To get a sense of what this can look like, in real-time, check out How Email Marketing + Automation + Copy = Increased ROI for SMBs.

Enhanced Personalization
Contrary to what many people think about AI, the thoughtful and strategic use of these tools can dynamically tailor email content, product recommendations, and customer experiences based on real-time data.

## Predict Campaign Performance with Greater Precision
Better (read: actually reliable) than a magic eight ball, AI can analyze past campaigns to forecast outcomes, helping you invest your marketing dollars more wisely.

Martech Consulting Co-Founder & CMO Keith Phillips breaks it down: "AI is not here to replace your marketing. It is here to make it smarter. For small businesses, AI in MarTech means faster insights, better targeting, and more time to focus on what really matters--serving your customers."

Again, AI isn't about complicating your marketing. It's about simplifying and enhancing it. With the right tools and guidance, small businesses can leverage AI to compete at a whole new level, without losing the human touch.

Some of you might have read an email from Keith earlier this summer in which he highlighted the benefits AI has personally afforded his own work-life balance by addressing the apprehension some people have when it comes to using it:

A lot of people were, and still are, hesitant about tools like automation and AI. Some feel like these things replace jobs. I get that concern. I value the dignity of human work, and replacing people was never the goal. It still is not. The purpose has always been to free up time, yours and your team's so you can focus on the work you love and the creative thinking that only humans can do.

One could argue Frederik Pohl, Nebula award winner, drew a parallel between his craft and the emergence of AI as a positive marketing disruptor:

> "The science fiction method is dissection and reconstruction. You look at the world around you, and you take it apart into all its components. Then you take some of those components, throw them away, and plug in different ones, start it up and see what happens. That's the method: restructure the world we live in in some way, then see what happens."

That may be the method, but a method is only as effective as the team managing it. Yielding the floor to Keith once again:

> "Maybe you are a chef who wants to spend less time on accounting and more time crafting recipes. Maybe you are a financial advisor who wants more time to help more families. Maybe you are a new business owner looking to grow, or a tired dad who just wants more time with his kids."
