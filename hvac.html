Common questions

Straight answers to
the obvious questions.

What does the $175/month cover exactly?
Your monthly covers hosting, SSL, security, and up to 2 content update requests per month. That means changing your phone number, updating your service area, swapping a service name, tweaking copy. Anything that takes us under 30 minutes. Bigger changes, new pages, or integrations are quoted separately.
Do I need to own a domain name already?
No. If you already have one, great. We'll connect the site to it. If you don't, we'll help you register one. Domain registration is usually around $15–$20/year and is a separate cost you pay directly to the registrar (not us).
What's the two-year minimum about?
SEO takes time. A website that's been live for 12–18 months ranks meaningfully better than one that was just published. The two-year term lets us actually deliver results, not just launch a site and walk away. We're not trying to lock you in. We're trying to make sure the thing works.
Will this actually help me show up on Google?
Yes, but to be honest with you, it takes a few months to build traction. We build the site with proper local SEO from the start (the right keywords, the right page structure, your service area built in), but Google needs time to index and trust a new domain. Most clients see meaningful improvement in 3–6 months.
I already have a website. Can you improve it instead?
Reach out and tell us what you've got. We can take a look and let you know whether a rebuild makes more sense than an overhaul. Sometimes it does, sometimes it doesn't.
What if I want to cancel before two years?
We'll discuss the terms when you sign on. The short version: the two-year commitment is real. We put significant time into building and supporting these sites, and the $175/month pricing only makes sense at that term length. That said, life happens. We're reasonable people and we'll work with you.
How involved do I have to be?
Minimal. We need your business info upfront (name, services, service area, phone, a few photos if you have them). After that, we handle everything. You'll review a draft before it goes live, and that's mostly it unless you want changes.
